About Me

I am a dedicated Biomedical Scientist with extensive expertise in toxicology, risk assessment, biochemistry, physiology, and medicine. Currently pursuing my PhD at the University of Saskatchewan's Toxicology Center, I focus on investigating complex environmental contamination and its health impacts.

My research integrates advanced bioinformatics approaches with traditional toxicological methods to understand the mechanisms of toxicity and develop evidence-based risk assessment strategies. I have a strong foundation in both laboratory research and teaching, with experience across multiple continents.

Research Focus

Bioinformatics & Genomics

Advanced analysis of metagenomics data to identify antimicrobial resistance genes and develop food safety protocols. Expertise in multimodal data integration combining genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics.

Risk Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of environmental contaminants and their health impacts. Specializing in dose-response relationships and target organ toxicity assessment in complex exposure scenarios.

Environmental Toxicology

Investigation of groundwater contamination effects on biological systems, with focus on neurotoxicity, hepatic toxicity, and systematic health impacts using rodent models.

Neurotoxicology

Assessment of cognitive and memory impacts from environmental contaminant exposure, utilizing advanced behavioral testing and cerebrovascular hemodynamics analysis.
